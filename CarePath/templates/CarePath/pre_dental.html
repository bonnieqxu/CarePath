Pre-treatment Dental Assessment



A dental assessment and treatments before your cancer treatments are crucial steps in your cancer care journey. By addressing dental issues early, we can prevent complications, reduce the risk of serious conditions like osteoradionecrosis, and maintain your oral health.


Importance of pre-radiotherapy dental assessment

1. Preventing Complications

Radiotherapy can weaken your immune system and reduce saliva production, making your mouth more susceptible to infections and decay. Addressing existing dental issues beforehand helps prevent these complications.


2. Reducing the Risk of Osteoradionecrosis (ORN)

ORN is a condition where the jawbone becomes damaged due to radiation. By treating dental problems such as infections or extractions before radiotherapy, we reduce the risk of developing ORN.


3. Maintaining Oral Health

Healthy teeth and gums are crucial for your comfort and quality of life. A dental assessment ensures that your mouth is in the best possible condition before treatment begins.


What to Expect During Your Dental Assessment

1. Comprehensive Examination

Your dentist will perform a thorough examination of your mouth, teeth, and gums. This may include X-rays to get a detailed view of your dental health.


2. Treatment Plan

Based on the examination, your dentist will create a personalised treatment plan. This plan may include cleanings, fillings, extractions, or other procedures to address any issues.


3. Preventive Care

Your dentist will provide advice on maintaining good oral hygiene during radiotherapy. This may include using fluoride treatments, special mouthwashes, and other tips for managing dry mouth and mucositis.



Common Dental Treatments Before Radiotherapy

1. Cleanings and Fillings

Professional cleanings remove plaque and tartar, reducing the risk of gum disease. Fillings repair cavities to prevent further decay.


2. Extractions

In some cases, teeth that are severely damaged or at risk of causing problems may need to be removed. This is done to prevent infections during and after radiotherapy, and to minimise the risk of Osteoradionecrosis (ORN).


3. Fluoride Treatments

Fluoride strengthens your teeth and helps protect against decay. Your dentist may recommend using fluoride gel or rinse regularly.


4. Tips for Maintaining Oral Health During Radiotherapy

Your dentist will also provide valuable information on how to keep your oral health in top shape during radiotherapy and offer tips on minimising common side effects.
