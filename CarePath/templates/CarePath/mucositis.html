Oral Mucositis and Dry Mouth Support Clinic



This clinic is available to patients receiving radiation treatment to the oral cavity or mouth. It runs simultaneously with your radiotherapy treatment and occurs each Thursday morning between 8.30 am to 12 pm. Your treatment team will refer you to this service.


Location:

Dental Clinic, Level 6, outpatients, Building 1, Auckland City Hospital. Follow the green line to building 1, take the orange bank lift to level 6 and turn left. Outpatient clinic is on the Mt Eden side of the building.


Purpose of this clinic

During your head and neck radiation treatment, with or without chemotherapy, you may experience mucositis (inflammation on your mouth lining) and xerostomia (dry mouth) as side effects. Mucositis occurs when cancer treatments damage the cells lining your mouth or other parts of the gastrointestinal tract (gut). Xerostomia is having a dry mouth. It happens when cancer treatment damages the salivary glands, so they make less saliva (spit).
You may experience symptoms like irritation, a stinging sensation, and pain. You may also notice changes in your taste and sensation, which can make food seem less enjoyable. Additionally, you could face challenges with swallowing, speaking, and opening your mouth.
To help you manage these issues and make your treatment as comfortable as possible, our dedicated team is here to support you. They will monitor your oral condition, pain management, speech, diet and physical health, and work with you to address any concerns and provide strategies to ease these side effects, ensuring you get the care and comfort you need throughout your treatment journey.


What to expect

You will have multiple visits to this clinic depending on the length of your radiotherapy treatment. Upon arrival, you will be asked to fill out a questionnaire at each visit. Your responses help us support you through any side effects from your radiation therapy. This clinic is run by a multidisciplinary team, you will be seen by a qualified Oral health therapist (OHT) or dentist, a Speech-Language Therapist, and Specialist Oncology Nurses. They will help you navigate oral health conditions during and following either surgery, radiotherapy, chemotherapy or a combination of these. They will assess your mouth and provide an oral care regime throughout your cancer treatments as you are more vulnerable to mouth infections.



Members of the team
Oral health therapist (OHT):

Qualified to works as both a dental hygienist and a dental therapist, providing periodontal (gum) care for all ages and overall dental care for children and adolescents.


Speech language therapist (SLT):

A speech and language therapist is a health professional who diagnoses and treats people with communication, eating, drinking and swallowing difficulties. Providing advice, information, education and support to people with communication and swallowing difficulties, their carers and their families/whanau.


Oncology nurse specialist:

Senior nurse that specialises in the Head and Neck treatment area. Available for clinical advice about your treatment, management of side effects, referrals to other clinical staff.


Dentist:

A registered health professional who examines, diagnoses, treats and advises patients on dental issues during treatment.
