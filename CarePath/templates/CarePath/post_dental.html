Post-treatment Dental Visits



Congratulations on completing your oncology treatment! As you move forward, taking care of your oral health is a key part of your recovery. You will soon have an appointment with our dental team, where we'll examine your mouth and address any remaining dental needs.

To allow your body time to adjust after radiotherapy, we'll arrange your dental visit approximately 6-8 weeks after your treatment. This will help ensure that your oral health is properly assessed and managed.

Location: Dental Clinic at Greenlane Clinical Centre
Address: Hospital and Specialists Dentistry, Level 1, Building 4, Greenlane Clinical Centre


What to Expect at Your Dental Appointment

During your dental appointment, you will usually see both a dentist and a dental hygienist. They work together to ensure that all aspects of your oral health are addressed. You might need a few visits to complete all the necessary treatments.
Here's what will typically happen:


1. Comprehensive Examination

X-rays: X-rays will be taken to provide a detailed view of your teeth and jaw.
Teeth and Gums: Although the dentist may have planned some treatments before your radiotherapy, they will reassess your teeth and gums, checking for any new issues.
Dry Mouth and Mucositis: They will evaluate any problems with dry mouth and check for signs of mucositis to see how it is healing.


2. Follow-Up Treatment

Based on their findings, the team will provide any needed follow-up treatment. This may include:
• Cleaning: A thorough cleaning to remove plaque and tartar.
• Fillings: Treatment for any cavities.
• Extractions: Removal of any problematic teeth if necessary.
• Dentures: Refer you to a prosthodontist for denture consideration if needed.


3. Discussion and Advice

The dentist and hygienist will:
• Discuss any concerns or issues you might have.
• Provide guidance on maintaining good oral hygiene.
• Offer advice on managing any side effects from your treatment.

If you have any questions or need more information, please feel free to ask during your appointment. We're here to support you and ensure your oral health is in the best possible condition.


Tips for Maintaining Your Oral Health

Maintaining good oral hygiene is essential for your overall health, especially after oncology treatment. Here are some helpful tips to keep your mouth in the best condition:

1. Have a Good Homecare Routine

• Brushing
o Brush Twice Daily: Brush your teeth every morning and night.
o Use a Soft Toothbrush: A soft bristle toothbrush is gentle on your gums and teeth.
o High Fluoride Toothpaste: Use a high fluoride toothpaste like Colgate NeutraFlour 5000 for added protection.
• Cleaning Between Teeth
o Daily Cleaning: Make sure to clean between your teeth every day.
o Floss or Interdental Brushes: Use floss or interdental brushes to remove food particles and plaque between your teeth.
• Denture Care
o Remove and Clean: Take out your dentures every night and clean them thoroughly. Denture cleaning tablets can help keep them fresh.


2. Managing Dry Mouth

Dry mouth is a common issue after treatment. Here's how to manage it effectively:
• Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
• Use Baking Soda Mouth Rinses: These rinses can help remove thick, sticky saliva and lower mouth acidity.
• Saliva Substitutes: Consider using products like Oral 7 to help manage dry mouth.
• Chew Sugar-Free Gum: This can stimulate saliva flow and help with dryness.


3. Dietary Advice

• Eat a Balanced Diet: Choose foods that are soft and easy to chew, and avoid very hot, spicy, or crunchy foods that may irritate your mouth's lining.
• Limit Sugary and Acidic Foods: Reduce consumption of sugary and acidic foods and drinks to lower the risk of cavities.
• Snack Wisely: If you snack frequently, rinse your mouth with water afterwards to help clean away food particles and reduce acidity.
• Minimize Caffeine and Alcohol: Avoid excessive caffeine and alcohol, and opt for alcohol-free mouthwash if needed.

By following these tips, you can maintain a healthy mouth and reduce the risk of complications. If you have any questions or need additional advice, please ask during your dental appointments. We're here to support you every step of the way.
